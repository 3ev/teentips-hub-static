My Experience of Living with a Parent with a Mental Illness

With: Rochelle Watson

Rochelle Watson, a young person who was supported by the Our Time Charity, shares her story of living with a parent with a mental illness. Rochelle shares her advice for young people who find themselves in a similar situation, how to navigate stigma and everyday discrimination, and how to deal with peer rejection and social isolation in the context of mental illness. This is an honest, thought-provoking conversation which we hope will offer some insight into the experiences of children and young people with mental illness in the family.

Parental Mental Illness

With: Dympna Cunnane

We invited Dympna Cunnane, a psychologist and the Chief Executive of the Our Time Charity, to discuss parental mental illness and how to support families in need. Dympna shares her advice for parents with poor mental health on how to talk to their children about their illness. We also discuss the challenges faced by children, how schools can help, and finally, what support parents and children need.
